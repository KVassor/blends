In linguistics, false cognates are pairs of words that may have similar pronunciation and meaning but different etymologies. For example, in English the term sheriff sounds similar to the Arabic term sharif. Both refer to legal offices, but have different geographical origins.

Such connections also exist in music, whereas two songs may be sonically comparable but are culturally independent.
